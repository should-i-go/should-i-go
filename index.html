Should I Go?

Travel recommendation for better planning

Overview

Problem Statement & Hypothesis

You and your friend agree to meet for drinks tonight at a new bar in the Lincoln Park neighborhood of Chicago. One hour and a $50 Uber bill later, you’re still not at the bar because traffic is gridlocked due to a large conference in town. If only you’d known! Our capstone project can help. e’ll combine data about upcoming “black swan” (rare) events with historical information about traffic patterns under similar events to recommend which neighborhoods to avoid and which to enjoy.

Persona & Use Case

Our targeted customer are urban dwellers aged 20-40 in Chicago. These are people who travel to restaurants and bars to meet up with friends and colleagues, and would use traffic predictions to decide which neighborhoods to avoid.

Value Proposition

There are a number of travel recommendation engines on the market but, to our knowledge, their forecasts are based on real-time traffic and best estimates under average conditions. In financial analysis, teams add perspective to financial forecasts by projecting funds’ behavior under rare, “black swan” events. We can do something similar, something new, in the travel space. This recommendation engine will illustrate what areas of the city will be clear, congested or grid locked (green, yellow or red) when special events come to town.

The Model ?

We generated two models. The first model considered each segment’s relationship with special events individually, and the second model defined special events’ impact based on the distance between a segment and the event. While both models are good fits, the first model gives a more precise view of the magnitude of special events on certain streets. Some of this precision is lost when we try to generalize to a distance-driven model.



Definition Adjusted R2 Range of Impact of Special Events on Daily Average Speed
SPEED ~ SegmentID + WEEKDAY + HasGame:SegmentID 0.9631 6 mph
SPEED ~ SegmentID + WEEKDAY + HasGame:EventDistance 0.9622 0.5 mph

Detecting Black Swans

No Game

On days with no games, the two models were virtually identical, with a correlation coefficient of 0.9999139.

Game

On days with with games, the two models are still very similar, with a correlation coefficient of 0.9945453, but some loss of fidelity begins to appear.
